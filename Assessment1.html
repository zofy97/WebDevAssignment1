Presidents of Ireland

The title of President of Ireland has existed since the enactment of the 1937 Constitution.

The President's official residence is Áras an Uachtaráin, located in the Phoenix Park in the heart of Dublin City.


The President is elected for a seven-year term by the electorate using the Single Transferable Vote. Any citizen of Ireland who has reached the age of thirty-five is eligible for election to the Presidency provided that he or she has been nominated by twenty Dáil deputies or four county councils.

Responsibilities of the President

According to the Constitution, the President has the following list of official responsibilities:
  • Appointing the Government,
  • Convening and dissolving Dáil Éireann,
  • Signing bills into law,
  • Representing the State in foreign affairs,
  • Acting as Commander-in-Chief of the Armed Forces, and
  • Granting pardons.

List of Presidents

There have been nine Presidents since the establishment of the office in 1938:
  1. Dr. Douglas Hyde (1938-1945),
  2. Seán T. O'Kelly (1945-1959),
  3. Éamon de Valera (1959-1973),
  4. Erskine Childers (1973-1974),
  5. Cearbhall Ó Dálaigh (1974-1976),
  6. Dr. Patrick Hillery (1976-1990),
  7. Mary Robinson (1990-1997),
  8. Mary McAleese (1997-2011),
  9. Michael D. Higgins (2011-present).
